"Explain your code line-by-line to a duck."

Have you ever been stuck on a bug for hours? Then you ask a friend for help, and halfway through explaining the problem, you suddenly shout "Wait! I found it!" before they even say a word?

That is Rubber Ducking. By forcing yourself to explain the logic out loud in simple terms, your brain slows down and finds the mistake.

How to do it:

  1. Get a rubber duck (or a toy, or a pet).
  2. Place it on your desk.
  3. Explain your code to it, line by line.
  4. When the duck looks confused, you've found your bug.
